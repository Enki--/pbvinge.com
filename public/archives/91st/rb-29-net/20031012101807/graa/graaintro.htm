section of our RB-29 web site. The contents of the pages
included in this section have little to do with WW II, the Korean War
or early Cold War history or comment.

In the recent years, we, Nell and Chuck Stone (operating under the name of CHARNELL, in Little Falls, MN), have operated an exhibit and program development service, based in our craft studio facility. During this period, at least 80 to 90% of our clients have been public service organizations. As we closed down our shop facility in these later years of life, we chose to turn the bulk of our woodworking, photo lab and exhibit fabrication equipment and supplies over to public service facilities here in Little Falls. (We retained our computer equipment so we can continue to develop our RB—29 web site.)

As part of our past efforts, we have accumulated a variety of education materials that have, in the past, been published in the form of workbooks, color slide lectures, video tapes, etc. Considering their lasting value and to insure that these efforts are not lost in the shuffle, we have converted these materials into Internet or CD capable programs and have installed them on our RB-29 web site, offering them without charge to educators, public service staff, and other interested parties. Any suggestions and comments by interested viewers will be most welcome. You may reach us via e-mail by

Regarding Permissions:

These programs were developed with “public service” being their main objectives. Clip art was obtained from publishers and modified, as needed, for our use. Photos used are largely our own products or those of others where permission has been granted. Intellectual materials were gathered from a variety of sources and modified where necessary to fit our needs, but in all cases were taken from the public service domain or, where necessary, permissions were obtained.
